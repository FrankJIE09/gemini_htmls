Major Lymph Node Regions

Select a group below to highlight or read structural details. Use the 3D canvas on the right to rotate $360^\circ$ and examine deep/superficial channels.

Superficial Group

Parotid Lymph Nodes

Situated in and around the parotid salivary gland. They receive afferent lymphatic vessels from the temporal region, upper eyelids, external acoustic meatus, and posterior cheek.

Submandibular Group

Submandibular Nodes

Nestled along the inferior border of the mandible. Crucial in dentistry as they drain the medial palpebral commissure, cheek, nose side, upper lip, and lateral lower lip.

Submandibular Group

Submental Nodes

Located between the anterior bellies of the digastric muscles. They receive lymph from the central lower lip, tip of the tongue, and floor of the mouth.

Occipital & Mastoid

Occipital & Retroauricular

Drains the scalp area behind the crown and lateral scalp regions. Vessels cascade directly downwards into deep cervical chains.

Deep Cervical Chain

Deep Cervical Nodes

Clustered along the internal jugular vein. They form the final drainage gateway for all lymphatic fluid of the head and neck before reaching the thoracic duct.
